What is carbon neutrality?

volume_up

Carbon neutrality is a state of net-zero carbon dioxide emissions. This can be achieved by balancing emissions of carbon dioxide with its removal or by eliminating emissions from society.

What are Carbon Offsets?

volume_up

A carbon offset is a reduction in emissions of carbon dioxide or other greenhouse gases made in order to compensate for emissions made elsewhere. Offsets are measured in tonnes of carbon dioxide-equivalent (CO2e). One tonne of carbon offset represents the reduction of one tonne of carbon dioxide or its equivalent in other greenhouse gases.

How is your carbon footprint calculated?

volume_up

We developed this carbon calculator to calculate carbon emmisions using many of our daily activities - such as using electricity, driving a car, or disposing of waste - cause emissions. Together these emissions make up a household's carbon footprint. The calculator estimates your footprint in three areas: home energy, transportation and waste. Everyone's carbon footprint is different depending on their location, habits, and personal choices.
